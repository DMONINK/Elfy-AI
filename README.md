# 🌙 Elfy

**A Discord bot that actually feels like a person.**

Not another boring "how can I assist you today" bot. Elfy ( inspired by [hihumanzone's Gemini-Discord-Bot](https://github.com/hihumanzone/Gemini-Discord-Bot) ) chats like a real friend, remembers *you* specifically, draws pictures on request, and has her own little dashboard you can control her from — no coding needed.

---

## ✨ What makes her cool

| | |
|---|---|
| 🧠 **Remembers you** | She keeps her own private memory of *you* — separate from everyone else, even in the same channel. |
| 💬 **Talks like a human** | Short, casual replies. No walls of text. |
| 🎨 **Draws pictures** | Just ask — "draw me a dragon" or "turn this photo into a painting." |
| 👑 **VIP mode** | Give your best friends a special relationship with her — she'll greet them and treat them differently than everyone else. |
| 🎛️ **Web dashboard** | Change her personality, settings, and more from your phone — zero code required. |
| 👋 **Welcomes new members** | Every new person gets a fresh, never-repeated AI greeting. |

---

## 🕹️ Try the commands

| Command | What it does |
|---|---|
| `/forget` | Wipe your history and start fresh (can even give her a custom persona!) |
| `/mymemories` | See exactly what she remembers about you |
| `/createthread` | Make a thread she'll chat in |
| `/setchat` | Pick her main channel |
| `/status` | Quick stats — uptime, servers, chats |
| `/botrestart` | Restart her (owners only) |
| `/help` | Get a quick cheat-sheet |

---

## 🚀 Getting it running

1. `pip install -r requirements.txt`
2. Add your Discord bot token + Gemini API key
3. Run `python main.py`
4. Done — she's online! 🎉

> ⚠️ One must-do: turn on **Server Members Intent** in the Discord Developer Portal, or she won't start.

Runs anywhere with Python 3.12 — Replit, a VPS, even a phone with Termux.

---

## 🛠️ Good to know

- Her dashboard is password-protected — nobody can peek at private chats without it.
- All her memories, settings, and VIPs survive restarts.
- A few small things are slightly out of date under the hood (like `/help`'s command list) — nothing that affects how she runs day-to-day.
